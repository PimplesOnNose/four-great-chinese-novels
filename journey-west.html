历史概述

Historical Overview

Author

吴承恩

Wu Cheng'en (c. 1500–1582), a Ming dynasty scholar and writer. While authorship is debated, he is traditionally credited with the canonical 1592 edition.

Period

16世纪 · 明代

Published in 1592 during the Ming dynasty, drawing from centuries of oral storytelling and folk traditions about Xuanzang's pilgrimage.

历史意义

Historical Significance

基于真实历史的奇幻叙事

Journey to the West is based on the actual journey of the Chinese monk Xuanzang (602–664), who traveled to India in the 7th century to seek Buddhist scriptures. While the novel is a work of fantasy, it retains the broad outline of Xuanzang's own account, embellishing it with folk tales and imagination.

The novel transforms a historical pilgrimage into a mythological adventure, creating one of the most enduring narratives in world literature. Its blend of Buddhist, Taoist, and Confucian philosophy with comedy, satire, and adventure has made it "arguably the most popular literary work in East Asia."

It is a cornerstone text of Eastern fiction: its stature in Asian literary culture may be compared with that of The Canterbury Tales or Don Quixote in European letters.

— Julia Lovell, Translator

多重意义的经典

The novel operates on multiple levels: as a comic adventure, a satire of Chinese bureaucracy, a source of spiritual reflection, and a rich allegory. Each character represents different aspects of human nature—Sun Wukong embodies the restless mind, Tang Sanzang the sincere heart, Zhu Bajie base desires, and Sha Wujing steady obedience.

This multi-layered approach has allowed the novel to speak to readers across centuries and cultures, finding new relevance in each era.

为何经久不衰

Why It Endures

太阳神猴的全球影响力

Sun Wukong, the Monkey King, has become one of the most recognizable characters in world literature. His influence extends far beyond China: he inspired the Dragon Ball series, the 2024 video game "Black Myth: Wukong," Lego Monkie Kid, and countless other adaptations across manga, anime, film, and television.

The character's blend of rebellion, wisdom, and humor resonates across cultures, making him a universal symbol of the trickster hero.

跨越时代的文化传承

The novel continues to inspire new adaptations and interpretations. The 2024 video game "Black Myth: Wukong" became a global phenomenon, introducing millions of players to the story. The novel's themes of perseverance, redemption, and spiritual growth remain timeless.

Its influence on the xianxia (immortal hero) literary genre, which combines martial arts with high fantasy, has shaped Chinese popular culture for centuries.

主要人物

Key Characters

孫

孙悟空

Sun Wukong · The Monkey King

Born from a stone, he learned 72 transformations and became the "Great Sage Equal to Heaven." His journey from rebellion to enlightenment is the novel's core.

唐

唐三藏

Tang Sanzang · The Monk

A Buddhist monk sent by Emperor Taizang to retrieve scriptures from India. His sincerity and compassion guide the pilgrimage.

豬

猪八戒

Zhu Bajie · Pigsy

A former heavenly marshal banished to earth as a pig. His love of food and women provides comic relief while representing base human desires.

沙

沙悟净

Sha Wujing · Sandy

A dependable and hard-working disciple who carries the luggage. He represents steady obedience and the importance of perseverance.
